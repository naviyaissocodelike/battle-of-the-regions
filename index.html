The Battle of the Regions brings together the brightest startups from D.C., Maryland, and Virginia in a high-stakes, single-elimination pitch competition designed to crown the DMV's top entrepreneurial talent. Competing for capital, visibility, and the title of regional champion, founders will go head-to-head until 10 founders compete on the DCSTW stage where only one startup emerges victorious.

Learn more about the organizations powering Battle of the Regions.

District Angels is a DMV-affiliated angel investing collective dedicated to making DC, MD, and VA a powerhouse of innovation through Education, Shared Infrastructure, and Community. Through our Fellowship Academy, Monthly Deal Rooms, and Investor Activation Series, we connect emerging and experienced angels to curated deal flow and live investment opportunities — keeping participation accessible and inclusive across the region.

Now in its eleventh year, DC Startup & Tech Week is the DMV's premier entrepreneurship platform, surfacing world-class founders and connecting them with the capital and community they need to scale. DCSTW unites over 17,000 entrepreneurs, investors, and industry leaders annually — and through Battle of the Regions, provides a stage for 36 of the region's most promising Seed and Series A companies to compete for the championship title.

Visit Website → LinkedIn

Inspire Access mobilizes philanthropic capital toward high-performing companies and investment funds led by underrepresented founders, or those that support underrepresented communities. Their vehicle enables donors to activate idle charitable capital and generate returns that fuel additional giving and greater impact — handled swiftly and seamlessly, with minimal fees.

Visit Website → LinkedIn

The Abell Foundation is a Baltimore-based philanthropic organization dedicated to improving health, economic, and educational outcomes so that all people can thrive. Through grants, research, and catalytic investments, Abell supports innovative solutions across education, workforce development, community development, and more — with a focus on addressing structural inequity across the region.
